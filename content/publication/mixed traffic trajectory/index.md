abstract: A number of models for car following have been proposed for homogeneous traffic and some fo these have been modified or adapted to represent mixed traffic conditions in India. Vehicle followinf behavior under mixed traffic is both complex and challenging and cannot be adequately captured by conventional lane-based following models and their variants. For example, the behavior of a subject vehicle in mixed traffic conditions depends on the longitudinal spacing and the relative speed of the lead and the subject vehicles. However, te vehicular interactions also depend on the lateral movements such as lateral spacing and lateral speed. Further, the response of the subject vehicle also dpeends on the types of vehicles involved and their maneuvers in surrounding space. This study aims to address some of these gaps in existing vehicle following models for mixed traffic. Mixed traffic trajectory data collected from the mid-block section of a six-lane divided urban arterial road in Chennai city was used for this study. From the data setm leader-follower vehicle pairs identified based on three different methods - influence area method, headway method and video data tracking are compared and more suitable method is chosen for futher analysis. Variation of driver behavior due to different factors such as follower’s speed, relative spacing, models was done for different leader-follower vehicle types. The results show that the model parameters vary with not only subject vehicle type but also leader-follower pairs. Also, there is a significant effect of factors like lateral position of vehicles and types of following behaviors. This study will find application in developing more realistic mixed traffic simulation models by including these factors.
